Objectively Correct Game Reviews

FPS Game Reviews

  • Call of Duty: Modern Warfare II

    Release Date: October 28, 2022
    Price: $69.99
    Platforms: PC, PS5, Xbox Series X/S

    The Call of Duty formula returns, this time shinier than ever. Modern Warfare II delivers smooth gunplay and Hollywood action sequences, but let’s be honest — it's a glorified rehash. Multiplayer? Addictive. Campaign? Flashy but forgettable. If you’ve played one, you’ve played them all... but you’ll still sink 50 hours in and love every killstreak.

  • Doom Eternal

    Release Date: March 20, 2020
    Price: $39.99
    Platforms: PC, PS4, PS5, Xbox Series X/S, Switch

    This is not your dad’s shooter. Doom Eternal is chaos incarnate — an unrelenting symphony of blood, demons, and metal. If you’re not sprinting, slashing, or glory killing, you’re doing it wrong. It's FPS combat refined to its most brutal essence, and it *slaps* harder than a cacodemon to the face.

  • Valorant

    Release Date: June 2, 2020
    Price: Free to Play
    Platforms: PC

    Valorant is what happens when Counter-Strike drinks an energy drink and starts casting spells. It’s sweaty, it’s strategic, and it’s a magnet for cracked headshot kings and voice-chat chaos. Some love it, some uninstall it in a fit of rage. Either way, it owns your evenings once you start.

  • Halo Infinite

    Release Date: December 8, 2021
    Price: Free Multiplayer / $59.99 Campaign
    Platforms: PC, Xbox Series X/S

    Master Chief’s comeback tour looks good, feels tight, and sounds like a war drum. The campaign starts strong but overstays its welcome in the open world. Multiplayer? S-tier. But 343 forgot to pack content. We’re still waiting for core features that should’ve been here in 2021. Potential? Massive. Execution? Meh.

  • Overwatch 2

    Release Date: October 4, 2022
    Price: Free to Play
    Platforms: PC, PS5, Xbox Series X/S, Switch

    It’s like Overwatch 1, but with a shiny coat of paint, fewer players, and more corporate baggage. Don’t get us wrong — it still has amazing hero design and satisfying team fights. But don’t call it a sequel. It’s Overwatch 1.5 with a battle pass. Fun? Yes. Revolutionary? Not even close.

  • Escape from Tarkov

    Release Date: Beta ongoing (since July 2017)
    Price: $44.99 (Standard Edition)
    Platforms: PC

    This isn’t a game. It’s a punishment simulator with guns. Tarkov is brutal, slow, and anxiety-inducing in the best and worst ways. Every raid could be your last. Every sound matters. If you survive — you feel like a god. If you don’t, you rage uninstall. And then reinstall an hour later.
